## Key Data Points — Army Combat Arms

### Infantry (CMF 11)
- **11B** (Infantryman) and **11C** (Mortarman) — both via 11X contract [cite: goarmy.com]
- OSUT: **22 weeks** at Fort Moore, GA — longest infantry OSUT in Army
- SOF path: **Option 40** → RASP (50-60% attrition) → Ranger Battalion → Ranger School → SFAS
- ASVAB: CO = 87

### Combat Engineers (CMF 12)
- **12B** (Combat Engineer) — OSUT 14 weeks, Fort Leonard Wood, MO
- **Sapper Leader Course**: 5 weeks (~50% attrition) — earns the Sapper Tab; mandatory before Ranger School in most units
- SOF path: Sapper → Ranger → **18C** (SF Engineer Sergeant)
- **12D** (Diver) — ST = 110; highly specialized, direct SOF support

### Field Artillery (CMF 13)
- **13F** (Fire Support Specialist / Forward Observer) — FA = 96; **most SOF-valuable non-infantry MOS**
- 13F embeds at platoon level with infantry, armor, and cavalry — NOT rear-echelon
- **JTAC qualified** — coordinates all fires: artillery, mortars, CAS, attack aviation, naval gunfire
- Ranger Regiment **actively recruits 13F** for JTAC role (Option 40 and RASP2 available)
- **13M** (HIMARS Crewmember) — most relevant artillery MOS in modern peer conflict
- ASVAB: FA = 93-96

### Air Defense (CMF 14)
- **14S** (SHORAD) — short-range air defense, Stinger/M-SHORAD/Avenger
- Growing relevance: counter-UAS is the fastest-expanding mission in modern warfare

### Armor / Cavalry (CMF 19)
- **19D** (Cavalry Scout) — OSUT 16 weeks; same SOF paths as 11B; Army Reconnaissance Course
- **19K** (M1 Armor Crewman) — OSUT 22 weeks; 4 crew positions (driver → loader → gunner → TC)
- 19D vs 11B: both can go Ranger and SF; 11B has more slots/schools in practice [cite: reddit.com/r/army]
- M1 Armor signing bonus: up to **$47,500** [cite: goarmy.com]

### SOF Pipelines From Combat Arms
| Starting MOS | Target SOF Unit | Path |
|---|---|---|
| 11B Option 40 | 75th Rangers | RASP (~50-60% attrition, 8 wks) |
| Any MOS | SF Green Berets | SFAS (35 days) → SFQC (12-18 months) |
| 12B | 18C (SF Engineer) | Sapper → SFAS → SFQC engineer track |
| 13F | Ranger Regiment | JTAC quals + RASP2 |
| 68W | 18D (SF Medic) | 68W → SFAS → 50-week SF medic course |
| Any MOS / 11B/19D | Delta Force (CAG) | SF or Rangers → 4+ years → OTC Selection |

## Key Data Points — Air Force PJ

### Pipeline (BMT to Maroon Beret: ~30 months)
1. BMT (7.5 wks)
2. SW Prep (8 wks) — water confidence is #1 predictor
3. Assessment & Selection (4 wks) — 2026 redesign
4. **Zulu Course NEW 2026** (16 wks) — advanced shooting, movement, comms
5. Combat Dive (6 wks)
6. Airborne (3 wks) + MFF (5 wks)
7. SERE (3 wks)
8. **MP3 — 39 weeks** (Nationally Registered Paramedic — same as civilian flight paramedic)
9. Pararescue Apprentice Course (22 wks, Kirtland AFB NM)

### Career Tracks After Pipeline
- **AFSOC Special Tactics Squadron** — deploys with SEALs, SF, Rangers, JSOC
- **ACC Rescue Squadron** — helicopter-centric, downed pilot recovery, disaster response

### Why PJ Has the Best Civilian Transition of Any AFSWC Path
- **NR-P credential** is immediately usable: flight paramedic ($70-120K), HEMS, critical care transport
- **Fire service**: combination of paramedic + physical fitness makes PJs extremely competitive
- **Medical school**: real patient contacts + combat trauma = strong application material
- **Federal LE**: FBI HRT, DEA SOG, USMS SOG all recruit PJ veterans
